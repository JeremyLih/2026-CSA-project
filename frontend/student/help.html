How to use the Adaptive Test Engine

Six quick steps that cover everything from signing in to reviewing your results.

1

Sign in with your school account

Use your @smus.ca Google account on the sign-in screen. No separate password to remember — one tap of the Google button and you're in.

2

Pick a test from the dashboard

The dashboard shows your recommended tests up top. Click any test card to begin, or open All Tests to browse the full catalog and filter by status.

3

Read the adaptive difficulty badge

Each question is tagged Beginner, Intermediate, or Advanced. The engine starts you at your current level and moves up or down based on how you answer.

4

Watch the timer — 30 seconds per question

You get 30 seconds to answer each question. When the timer hits zero, the test auto-submits that question and moves on. There's no penalty for running out — it just counts as "no answer."

5

Follow the difficulty-path dots

The row of dots at the bottom of the test shows how your path has adapted: the higher the dot, the harder the question. It's a quick visual of where the engine has taken you so far.

6

Review past results any time

Head to Test History to see every test you've taken, or open a specific attempt from the Recent Activity panel on your dashboard.

Frequently asked questions

Tap any question to see the answer.

What happens if I run out of time on a question?
The question is submitted as "no answer" and counted as incorrect. The test then moves on to the next question automatically — you won't be stuck on one screen.
Can I go back to a previous question?
No. Because the engine adapts to each answer you submit, questions are locked in once you move on. This keeps the difficulty calibration fair for everyone.
Why did the difficulty change after my answer?
That's the adaptive engine at work. Answer correctly and the next question is harder; miss one and the next is a little easier. The goal is to find the hardest level you can reliably handle.
What does my adaptive level mean?
Your adaptive level (1–5) is a running estimate of your skill across the topics you've been tested on. Level 4 means you're consistently answering intermediate-to-advanced questions correctly. It updates after every test.
Can I retake a test?
Usually yes, unless your teacher has locked a specific attempt. Each retake generates a fresh question set, and your most recent attempt is the one that shows on the dashboard.
Who can see my results?
You and your teacher. Classmates can't see your scores, and aggregate stats shared with the class are always anonymized.
What if the site doesn't work or I see an error?
First, try refreshing the page. If you're mid-test, your progress is auto-saved, so you won't lose your answers. If the issue persists, message your teacher using the Contact form below.
What if I accidentally sign out during a test?
Don't panic — sign back in and open the same test. Your progress up to the last answered question is saved server-side, and you'll resume right where you left off.
